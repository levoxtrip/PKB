INTERESTS ‚Ä¢ What do I want here? ‚Ä¢ What does the other side want? (Pro tip: Consider interests of each key stakeholder) ‚Ä¢ What are the common interests?

FACTUAL & FINANCIAL RESEARCH ‚Ä¢ What are market prices? ‚Ä¢ What do experts say? ‚Ä¢ What are cultural norms and legal constraints?

OPTIONS ‚Ä¢ Brainstorm possible deal terms (list 6+ creative options).

RAPPORT ‚Ä¢ How can I be hard on the problem and soft on the person? ‚Ä¢ How can I set the right tone from the start? ‚Ä¢ Roleplay With friends or family

EMPATHY & ETHICS ‚Ä¢ How do things look from the other side? ‚Ä¢ How do they fit into their organization? What are the unique challenges of their role? ‚Ä¢ What might their ethical or spiritual considerations be?

SETTING & SCHEDULING ‚Ä¢ Where and when will you negotiate? ‚Ä¢ How important is discretion? Should negotiations be held in private or public? ‚Ä¢ What, if any, deadline will you face?

ALTERNATIVES TO AGREEMENT ‚Ä¢ What will you do if there's no deal? Knowing this will give you confidence to walk away. ‚Ä¢ Rank your alternative options.

WHO ‚Ä¢ Who can help? ‚Ä¢ Whose agreement must I win? ‚Ä¢ Who might be a decision-maker outside of view? (Spouse, boss, etc.)

INDEPENDENT CRITERIA ‚Ä¢ What are the objective benchmarks and industry standards? (Ex: Kelly Blue Book value) ‚Ä¢ These let you say. "Don't take my word for it; let's turn to something we both trust."

TOPICS, TARGETS, & TRADEOFFS ‚Ä¢ Topics (List agenda items like salary, hours, vacation time, etc.) ‚Ä¢ Targets (For each topic, write down your best target and walk away target) ‚Ä¢ Tradeoffs (Prioritize your targets, so you're prepared if you need to make tradeoffs)
